The Collection

The Collection Committee of the Society is a volunteer group that:
  • Maintains the records of the collection (the databases, lists and inventory of the collection)
  • Investigates potential gifts of artifacts to the collection.
  • Documents the addition of artifacts and removal of artifacts in and out of the collection records.
  • Tracks the movment of the artifacts, in and out of displays and storage areas.
The addition and removal of artifacts in the collection must be approved by the Society Board of Directors on recommendation from the collection committee chairperson.
The collection itself is maintained by volunteer members of the society. The preservation and maintenance of the individual artifacts in the collection is provided by various members of the society. These wonderful volunteers provide a cleaning before the season, spot cleaning during the season and closing at the end of the season. This provides an opportunity to view the artifacts in the building for early detection of potential problems.
Volunteers for the collection committee are always welcome. The current chairperson is James Graves, who can be reached at 315-524-4731. Jim has several projects ongoing:
  • Inventory of the over 7000+ items in the collection to verify location and condition. The process includes identifying duplicate or damaged items,
  • Locating the many artifacts in the museum that are not recorded in the database. Photgraphing, researching (ownership, donor, provenance, etc), recording the information into the database.
  • Continuing to photograph the artifacts for inclusion into the database. This long term project is only about 55% completed.
  • Maintaining the database, using the Open Source database software, LibreOffice.
